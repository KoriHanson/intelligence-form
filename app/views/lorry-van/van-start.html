Report a van or Light Goods Vehicle driver or business

How to report a driver or their business to the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) if you think they're breaking safety rules.

What you can report to DVSA

You can report a van or Light Goods Vehicle driver or the business running the vehicles (a ‘vehicle operator’) to DVSA if you think they’re breaking safety rules or conditions of their driving or operator licence.

This includes things like:

  • breaking drivers’ hours rules
  • overloading vehicles
  • using vehicles that are not safe to drive (unroadworthy)
  • using emission cheat devices
  • carrying dangerous or hazardous goods without permission

Reporting something anonymously

If you report something anonymously:

  • you’ll never be asked for your name or to provide contact details
  • your call will not be traced
  • you will not have to make a statement or be called as a witness
  • you will not have to appear in court

Depending on what you’re reporting, it might be possible for the MOT tester or garage to work out that you reported it - even if you report it anonymously.

If you do give your contact details

You can remain anonymous. However, if you give DVSA your contact details you might:

  • be contacted for more information
  • be asked to provide a statement
  • have to act as a witness and appear in court
